Famous Islamic Heritage Sites

Al-Hakim Mosque

The construction of the mosque began during the reign of the Fatimid Caliph Al-Aziz Billah in 380 AH/990 AD, and was completed by his son, Caliph Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah, in 403 AH/1013 AD, It follows the layout of congregational mosques, It consists of a central open courtyard surrounded by four porticos, which are structural supports holding arches that support the wooden roof.

Read more

Saladin Citadel

Saladin began its construction between 572 AH/1176 AD and 579 AH/1183 AD, but it was not completed during his lifetime, It was completed by Sultan Al-Kamil ibn Al-Adil in 604 AH / 1207 AD, and it remained the seat of power in Egypt until the reign of Khedive Ismail.

Read more

Al-Azhar Mosque

It was built by Jawhar al-Siqilli, and the first Friday prayer in Ramadan was held there in the year 361 AH/972 AD. It was named Al-Azhar after Lady Fatima al-Zahra, Its initial layout consisted of an open courtyard surrounded by three porticos. Renovations, additions, and restorations continued during the Fatimid period and in the eras that followed.

Read more
Sultan Al-Zahir Baybars Mosque

It is considered one of the oldest mosques of the Mamluk era. Its construction was begun by Al-Zahir Baybars Al-Bunduqdari (658-676 AH / 1260-1277 AD) On an area of approximately three feddans (12,600 square meters), in the year 665 AH / 1266 AD, and it continued to host religious rituals for a long period.

Read more

Gayer-Anderson Museum

The Gayer-Anderson Museum is one of the most important Islamic monuments in Egypt, distinguished by its oriental architectural design. The museum was originally composed of two separate houses, built during the Ottoman era, adjacent to each other.

Read more

Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art is the largest museum of Islamic antiquities in the world, displaying diverse collections of Islamic art from Egypt, India, China, Andalusia, the Levant, the Arabian Peninsula, North Africa, and other parts of the Islamic world. The idea of establishing it began during the reign of Khedive Ismail in 1869.

Read more

Sultan Al-Ghuri Agency

It is a commercial building that was used to house incoming merchants, to store and sell goods, and to conduct business deals, For storing and selling goods and conducting commercial transactions. The Sultan Al-Ghuri Agency is located on Al-Tablita Street in the Al-Azhar district and is also known as the Nakhlah and Al-Masbagha Agency.

Read more

Mrs. Wasila's house

Its construction dates back to the Ottoman era, by the brothers Abdul Haq and Lutfi Muhammad al-Kinani in 1074 AH/1664 AD, Lady Wasila Khatoun was its last resident since the beginning of the 19th century AD.The entrance of the house overlooks Al-Sitt Wasila Alley.

Rawda Island Nilometer

It was built in 247 AH/861 AD by order of the Abbasid Caliph Al-Mutawakkil on the island of Rawda,It was used to measure the flood level, allowing the determination of the amount of incoming water during the flood.

Read more

Al-Jeddawi Agency

It was built by Hassan Bek Al-Jadawi in 1207 AH/ 1792 AD, who was named after him because he took over the Emirate of Jeddah in 1184 AH/ 1770 AD, The Jadawi Agency is considered evidence of the economic prosperity that the city of Esna experienced during the 12th AH / 18th AD century.

Read more

Qaitbay Citadel

It was built by Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay Al-Mahmudi between 882 AH/1477 AD and 884 AH/1479 AD. The citadel is located in the Anfoushi district- Formerly Pharos Island – West Alexandria, consisting of a rectangular area surrounded by the sea on three sides. The citadel has a main entrance on the southwest side in the form of two towers.

Jamal al-Din house

The golden entity of Jamal al-Din “Shahbandar” the Great in Egypt in the year 1047 AH/ 1637 AD, located in Khashqadam Alley (Hawsh Qadam) Branching off from Al-Muizz li-Din Allah Street in the Al-Ghuriya area of Downtown Cairo, The house is considered one of Egypt's significant historical buildings
