Famous Islamic Heritage Sites

Al-Hakim Mosque

The construction of the mosque began during the reign of the Fatimid Caliph Al-Aziz Billah in 380 AH/990 AD, and was completed by his son, Caliph Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah, in 403 AH/1013 AD.

Read more

Saladin Citadel

Saladin began its construction between 572 AH/1176 AD and 579 AH/1183 AD, but it was not completed during his lifetime.

Read more

Al-Azhar Mosque

It was built by Jawhar al-Siqilli, and the first Friday prayer in Ramadan was held there in the year 361 AH/972 AD. It was named Al-Azhar after Lady Fatima al-Zahra.

Read more
Sultan Al-Zahir Baybars Mosque

It is considered one of the oldest mosques of the Mamluk era. Its construction was begun by Al-Zahir Baybars Al-Bunduqdari (658-676 AH / 1260-1277 AD).

Read more

Gayer-Anderson Museum

The Gayer-Anderson Museum is one of the most important Islamic monuments in Egypt, distinguished by its oriental architectural design.

Read more

Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art is the largest museum of Islamic antiquities in the world, displaying diverse collections of Islamic art from Egypt, India, China.

Read more

Sultan Al-Ghuri Agency

It is a commercial building that was used to house incoming merchants, to store and sell goods, and to conduct business deals.

Mrs. Wasila's house

Its construction dates back to the Ottoman era, by the brothers Abdul Haq and Lutfi Muhammad al-Kinani in 1074 AH/1664 AD

Rawda Island Nilometer

It was built in 247 AH/861 AD by order of the Abbasid Caliph Al-Mutawakkil on the island of Rawda

Al-Jeddawi Agency

It was built by Hassan Bek Al-Jadawi in 1207 AH/ 1792 AD, who was named after him because he took over the Emirate of Jeddah in 1184 AH/ 1770 AD.

Qaitbay Citadel

It was built by Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay Al-Mahmudi between 882 AH/1477 AD and 884 AH/1479 AD. The citadel is located in the Anfoushi district.
